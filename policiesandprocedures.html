Piano Studio Policy : 2018 - 2019

Lessons

Weekly lessons provide guidance and an opportunity for parents, students, and teacher to ask questions. Regular attendance is important and students must arrive on time. If a student arrives late, their lesson will still end at the scheduled time. There is no refund for a shortened lesson due to a student’s tardiness. Depending on the age and ability level of the student, lessons will range from thirty (30) minutes to an hour. Parents are welcome and invited to sit in on lessons. Concentration is essential to a beneficial lesson and if there is excessive distraction, I will ask for visitors to leave. Students’ friends should not attend lessons without advance notice. Students must bring all materials to lessons each week. Lesson time is wasted and progress stunted having to search for replacement materials. If this becomes a regular pattern of behavior, students will be excused from lessons. Lessons will cover a variety of areas including technical skills, repertoire, theory, sight- reading, improvisation, Communicationand music history.

Communication

If you need to speak with me personally, please do so at the beginning of your child’s lesson time so as not to interrupt another student’s lesson.

Summer Lessons

Students are strongly urged to take advantage of Summer lessons in June and July. These ensure that progress made during the year is not lost. Individual students who take the equivalent of six regular-term lessons will receive priority scheduling for Fall. Registration for summer lessons takes place online and you are in control of when they happen.

“Practice only on the days you eat.”

Practicing is the backbone to every musician’s success. Please make piano practice a part of the daily routine in the same way as math and reading homework. A quiet and comfortable space at home, free from distractions, will provide the best environment for good practice.

Individual practice suggestions will be made but a general rule is daily practice for the length of the weekly lesson (i.e. 30 minute less, 30 minutes of practice per day, 5-7 days a week).

Please do not turn practicing into punishment. This is the quickest way to sour a music to student study. Let me know if there is difficulty getting a student to practice. Practice MUST be done between lessons for any progress to be made. Younger students will need more parental involvement to develop a healthy practice routine. If a student does not develop a regular practice habit I reserve the right to dismiss them from the studio.

The Home Instrument

The instrument at home must be key in tune and have a registered piano technician tend to it at least once or twice a year, preferably as the weather changes. Practicing on an out of tune or poorly functioning instrument can prove difficult, unrewarding, and eventually lead to a desire to quit piano altogether. Acoustic pianos are an investment but will provide the most rewarding progress. Once the decision to invest in an acoustic piano has been made I am happy to help in the process of choosing an appropriate instrument.

A digital keyboard is acceptable as long as it has 88 weighted keys and is kept at an appropriate height, on a sturdy stand. The keyboard should not be placed on a bed, table, or the floor. Toys and keyboards that are not full size will create difficulties and make proper, healthy technique impossible to establish. All reasonable effort should be made to transition to an acoustic piano as progress occurs.

Recitals

There are two recitals each year, Fall and Spring. Please make them a priority. The recital offers an opportunity to show progress, build confidence, and cultivate stage presence. With the exception of beginning students and those playing duets/chamber music, all students’ performances will be memorized and performed without music.

The student and I will choose recital repertoire. Once the music is chosen it cannot be changed. Doing so abbreviates the time we have to truly learn the music and puts the student at a disadvantage.

The Hands

Keep nails trimmed and hands clean.

Washing hands before lessons keeps the instrument clean and prevents the passage of germs from student to student.

If a student suffers an injury to a hand or arm, do not fret! Piano lessons can continue with one hand alone and adjustments made to the curriculum until students are medically released to use both hands/arms. I will never go in opposition to a doctor’s orders for best healing.
